User Centered Design Projects

Jefit IOS Redesign

Jefit is a mobile fitness application that enables users to track their progress, find and build custom routines, and connect with the fitness community to stay motivated towards a healthy lifestyle. My team and I set out to improve the design of the IOS application in order to create a better fitness experience. We utilized market research and a cognitive walkthrough to identify major usage frustrations. After, we iteratively designed and tested to create a great Jefit experience.

Contextual Information and Communication

Our team was interested in analyzing mobile contextual communication and information presentation. We researched and designed a future prototype for the Facebook Home lock screen which contextually presents information and contact meta information while traveling from home to a restaurant. We did an extensive literature review and created a survey determining what users want within certain contexts, what information they need and are willing to share in order to better communicate, and user privacy concerns regarding contextual systems.

Personal Projects

E X I S T

E X I S T is an asynchronous, anonymous message board in which you can post anything! When one visits the site, they will see the most recent four posts that users created and a new quote generated by me daily. If someone chooses, they can make a post and push out the fourth most recent post. The site's idea is to create an existential experience - the site absurdly exists for no reason other than I wanted it; anyone can leave their mark, no matter how impactful; in the end, their mark is only ephemeral.

I am tracking the posts people make in relation to the daily quote and time of day in a database. I want to expand E X I S T so anyone can host and update their quote of the day!
